❓ Dutch Question Words — Mastery Guide

Vraagwoorden: learn them, drill them, test them CBT-style

"CBT" here = computer-based test practice, matching how the real DUO A2 exams work (Luisteren, Spreken, Schrijven are all done on a computer). The quiz below is styled the same way: one question at a time, multiple choice, immediate right/wrong feedback — no going back, just like exam day.

🪜 7-step mastery method

1
Group by answer type Day 1 — 15 min

Don't memorize the list top to bottom. Group them: person (wie, met wie, van wie), place (waar, waarnaartoe, waarvandaan), time (wanneer, hoe laat, hoe lang, hoe vaak), reason (waarom), manner/quantity/choice (hoe, hoeveel, welke). This mirrors how your brain needs to react on the real exam — you hear a question and instantly know what kind of answer to give.

2
Flashcard recognition drill Day 1–2 — 10 min/day

Make flashcards: Dutch word → English meaning, and reverse. Drill until instant recall (under 2 seconds). Use Anki/Quizlet so it repeats the ones you get wrong more often.

3
Fill-in-the-blank sentences Day 2 — 15 min

Take 15 example sentences with the question word removed, and fill them in from memory. This is the "Writing" skill — it forces you to recall the word, not just recognize it.

4
Listening drill: question word → expected answer Day 3 — 15 min

Have someone (or a text-to-speech tool) read questions aloud. Your job: don't answer the question yet — just say out loud what kind of answer is expected ("a place", "a time", "a reason"). This trains the split-second parsing you need for the Luisteren exam, where you often only hear the audio once.

5
Speaking drill: full answers Day 3–4 — 15 min

Now answer the same questions out loud in full sentences, recorded. E.g. Q: "Waarom leer je Nederlands?" → A: "Ik leer Nederlands omdat ik in Nederland woon." Aim for the 3-part A2 answer structure: direct answer + one detail/reason + (optional) example.

6
Writing drill: compose your own questions Day 4 — 15 min

Write 10 original questions of your own, one per question word, about a form-filling context (name, address, date of birth, reason for visit). This mirrors the Schrijven exam's form-filling task, which often expects you to understand or produce this exact type of question.

7
Full CBT-style mock quiz Day 5 onward — 10 min, repeat until 100%

Use the quiz below. Do it timed, no pausing, no re-reading — exactly like the real computer exam. Repeat daily until you consistently score 100%, then only revisit it once every few days to keep it fresh.
